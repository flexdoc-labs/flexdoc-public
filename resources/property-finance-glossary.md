# Property Finance Glossary

This glossary explains common terms used in property finance.

It is general information only.

## AIP

Approval in principle. A preliminary indication from a lender that a borrower may qualify for finance, subject to conditions and formal assessment.

## Bare trust

A trust structure commonly used in SMSF property finance where legal title is held by a custodian or bare trustee.

## Borrowing capacity

An estimate of how much a borrower may be able to borrow based on lender assessment.

## Contribution

Money added to a superannuation fund. Contributions may be concessional or non-concessional and may affect SMSF liquidity and lending assessment.

## Credit assistance

Help provided to a client in relation to applying for, structuring or selecting credit.

## Equity release

Borrowing against existing property equity for an approved purpose.

## LRBA

Limited recourse borrowing arrangement. A structure that may allow an SMSF to borrow to acquire an asset, subject to legal and regulatory requirements.

## LVR

Loan-to-value ratio. The loan amount expressed as a percentage of the property value.

## SMSF

Self-managed superannuation fund. A private superannuation fund managed by its members or directors of a corporate trustee.

## Servicing

A lender's assessment of whether the borrower or fund can meet loan repayments.

## Valuation

A lender-approved assessment of property value used for credit assessment.
