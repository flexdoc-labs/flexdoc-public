# SMSF Property Finance Explainer

SMSF property finance is a specialised lending area where a self-managed superannuation fund may borrow to purchase property through a limited recourse borrowing arrangement.

This page is a general educational resource only.

## Key concepts

### Self-managed superannuation fund

An SMSF is a private superannuation fund managed by its members or directors of a corporate trustee.

### Limited recourse borrowing arrangement

An LRBA is a structure that allows an SMSF to borrow for a single acquirable asset, with lender recourse generally limited to that asset.

### Bare trust

A bare trust is commonly used to hold legal title to the property while the SMSF has the beneficial interest.

### Contributions

SMSF contributions can affect fund liquidity and lending assessment.

Lenders may consider:
- contribution history
- fund balance
- liquidity buffers
- income available to the fund
- proposed future contributions

## Common friction points

SMSF property finance often involves:
- accountants
- solicitors
- lenders
- brokers
- trustees
- financial advisers where appropriate

Common friction points include:
- wrong purchasing entity
- late bare trust setup
- unclear contribution position
- incomplete documentation
- lender policy differences
- valuation and settlement timing
